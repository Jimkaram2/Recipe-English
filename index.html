Yiayia's Traditional Greek Pastitsio

Personal Connection

Every time I return to Greece with my family, there is one tradition that never changes, sitting around my grandmother's table to enjoy her homemade pastitsio. The warm, comforting aroma of cinnamon and béchamel fills her home, welcoming us back in a way that words cannot. This dish isn't just food; it's a symbol of home, heritage, and love. Watching my Yiayia cook, hearing her stories, and sharing the meal together are moments that make me feel deeply connected to my roots.

Eating pastitsio is reliving memories of laughter-filled evenings, bonding with family, and embracing our Greek culture. The dish reminds me of my identity and symbolizes my resilience in maintaining tradition. Whenever I take a bite, I’m reminded of the strength and love that shape my family, and it inspires me to cherish these moments and carry them forward.

Ingredients

  • For the Meat Sauce:
    • 2 tbsp olive oil
    • 1 large onion, finely chopped
    • 2 cloves garlic, minced
    • 500g ground beef
    • 1/2 cup red wine
    • 400g crushed tomatoes
    • 2 tbsp tomato paste
    • 1 tsp ground cinnamon
    • 1/2 tsp ground cloves
    • Salt and pepper to taste
  • For the Pasta Layer:
    • 500g bucatini or tubular pasta
    • 2 tbsp butter
    • 2 large eggs, lightly beaten
    • 1 cup grated kefalotyri or Parmesan cheese
  • For the Béchamel Sauce:
    • 100g butter
    • 1/2 cup all-purpose flour
    • 4 cups warm milk
    • Pinch of ground nutmeg
    • Salt and pepper to taste
    • 2 large eggs, lightly beaten

Instructions

  1. Prepare the meat sauce by sautéing onion and garlic, adding beef, and simmering with wine, tomatoes, and spices.
  2. Cook pasta, then combine with butter, eggs, and half of the cheese.
  3. Make the béchamel by whisking butter and flour, gradually adding milk, seasoning, and finishing with eggs.
  4. Layer half the pasta, the meat sauce, remaining pasta, and top with béchamel and cheese.
  5. Bake at 180°C (350°F) for 45-50 minutes until golden brown.
  6. Rest for 15 minutes before serving.
